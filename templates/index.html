WebGap Quick Reference Guide
The Personalized Opener Rule
NEVER use a generic opener like "I came across your business and was impressed."
Your opener MUST reference something specific: years in business, a phrase from a review, a recurring theme customers mention.
Specific = believable. Generic = deleted.
Subject Line Strategy
BEST: "I made something for [Name]" — specific, unexpected, creates curiosity without revealing the pitch.
BOLD: "You don't have a website, so I built one for you" — highest open rate, use only for businesses with zero online presence.
WARM/SAFE: Use when approaching businesses that may be more formal or if BEST feels too bold.
Website Prompt Pro Tips
Upload minimum 5 review screenshots to landingsite.ai — the more emotional and specific, the better.
Add the vibe field if reviews suggest an atmosphere: "like a spa but for nails", "feels like a members club".
Add brand colors if visible on their social media — prevents generic blues and greys.
After generation: check CTA appears 3x, 2-3 review phrases in copy, mobile looks premium.
What Makes a Site Actually Convert
Hero headline speaks to a FEELING, not just the service. "Walk In. Walk Out Confident." beats "Best Barbershop in Dallas."
Real review quotes in the copy — visitors trust peers, not pitches.
A single, clear primary CTA that appears multiple times. Not three different actions — one.
Mobile-first: most business owners will view on their phone the moment you send the link.
Follow-Up Rules
Send one follow-up only — after 4-5 days of no reply.
After the follow-up, move on. Chasing kills the warm tone you built.
If they say "not right now" — snooze 30 days and revisit. Circumstances change.
Upsell Sequence (after close)
Don't upsell during the initial sale. The website is the door opener.
Once the site is live and they love it: Google Business Profile setup, social media links, monthly maintenance, review generation system.
